# 07 — How to Read a COA

## Section heading (H2)
```
How to Read a Certificate of Analysis
```

## Intro
```
A COA is straightforward to interpret once you know what each section is telling you. Use this as a quick reference when reviewing any certificate.
```

## Reading guide
Render as a clean checklist or annotated layout.

- **Find the lot identifier first.** Confirm the certificate corresponds to the specific batch you are evaluating. A COA without a matching lot reference cannot be tied to the material.
- **Check the identity result.** Look for the mass spectrometry confirmation and whether the measured molecular weight matches the expected value.
- **Read the purity figure with its method.** A purity percentage should be paired with the method that produced it (typically RP-HPLC). The method gives the number its meaning.
- **Examine the chromatogram.** A dominant single peak with minimal additional peaks supports the stated purity. The visual trace is part of the evidence, not decoration.
- **Review the impurity profile.** Detected impurities provide context. Their presence and quantification are a sign of thorough analysis, not a flaw in the document.
- **Note the analytical methods listed.** Documented methods are what make a result interpretable and, where appropriate, independently checkable.

## What good documentation looks like (paragraph)
```
Strong documentation is specific, method-anchored, and batch-tied. It states what was measured, how it was measured, and which lot the measurement describes. When all three are present, the certificate functions as verifiable evidence rather than a general assurance.
```
